# Analysis: Bridging the silos - Episode 38, Tetradian on Architectures

## Executive Summary
The video discusses using Enterprise Architecture to bridge organizational silos rather than breaking them, emphasizing that silos form naturally from differing jargon, perspectives, and histories. It advocates for purposeful connections through tools like VPEC-T and building trust via in-person relationships to enhance collaboration without disrupting specialized groups.

## Key Takeaways
- Silos arise naturally from each discipline's unique jargon, perspectives, histories, and views, and attempting to break them risks damaging the organization.
- Enterprise Architecture should focus on **bridging silos** by connecting dots across clinical and business areas that groups might not see independently.
- Use the **VPEC-T framework** (Values, Policies, Events, Content, Trust) to map potential clashes between groups and identify bridging strategies.
- The enterprise architect's role is inherently political, requiring time to build trust and social networks through in-person relations rather than imposing solutions.
- Core principle: "Things work better when they work together **on purpose**," prioritizing effective, intentional connections over forced integration.
