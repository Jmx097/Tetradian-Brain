# Analysis: An enterprise architect's notes on coronavirus: Tetradian on Architectures

## Executive Summary
The speaker, an enterprise architect at Heathrow Airport, shares real-time personal disruptions from a mandatory 14-day quarantine policy amid the coronavirus outbreak, using it as a lens to critique poorly designed systems and architectures that fail under chaos. He highlights how new technologies enable contactless work like video recording, while emphasizing the need for better architectural design to handle impossible requirements. This serves as a wake-up call for enterprise architectures, with a note that he may be offline due to travel and health risks.

## Key Takeaways
- Personal plans (hotel stays, travel, campervan rental) are disrupted by quarantine rules lacking practical implementation guidance, exposing structural flaws in policy and systems.
- Architectures and systems often poorly anticipate real-world chaos, expecting individuals to resolve inherent design errors or "slips through the cracks."
- New technologies, such as solo video recording and publishing, enable communication and work without physical contact, providing resilience in uncertain times.
- This situation acts as a "useful wake-up call" for rethinking four key points in enterprise architectures to better handle uncertainty and impossible demands.
