# Analysis: Enterprise architecture and coronavirus: Trust: Tetradian on Architectures

## Executive Summary
The video examines how the coronavirus pandemic has exposed critical trust issues in organizations, governments, and information sources, emphasizing that trust is a foundational metric for any business. It highlights causes of trust erosion—such as fake news, delayed facts, and lack of transparency—and urges enterprise architects to address these by fostering honesty and reliable information flows. Rebuilding trust through clear communication and motives is essential for cooperation and organizational resilience.

## Key Takeaways
- Every organization depends on **trust** as a core element, yet it is rarely measured as a key metric, making its loss devastating to business operations.
- Coronavirus has amplified trust breakdowns, including skepticism toward governments, experts, facts versus fake news, and hidden intentions of leaders or entities.
- Key trust erosion factors include delayed or absent information (e.g., infection lag times), lack of honesty/transparency, and uncertainty in critical data like disease transmissibility.
- Trust is rebuilt via **honesty, transparency, and accurate information**, enabling cooperation based on shared faith in outcomes, intentions, and motives.
- Enterprise architects should apply these trust principles routinely to ensure organizations are trustworthy, using pandemic lessons for stronger architecture.

## Actionable Insights / Tutorials
- Measure **trust explicitly** as a core organizational metric to monitor and maintain business viability.
- Address information lags by developing systems for timely, trackable data (e.g., identifying infections despite 7-10 day delays).
- Promote **honesty and transparency** in communications to rebuild trust—provide accurate updates even in uncertainty, avoiding "floundering with no information."
- Enterprise architects: Explore trust dynamics in architecture design to ensure reliability in routine scenarios (e.g., flight delays) and crises, fostering cooperation through trusted motives and outcomes.
