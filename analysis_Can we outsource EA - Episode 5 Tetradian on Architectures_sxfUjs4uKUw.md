# Analysis: Can we outsource EA? - Episode 5, Tetradian on Architectures

## Executive Summary
Enterprise Architecture (EA) cannot be outsourced as it is an ongoing organizational practice requiring internal ownership, personal responsibility, and contextual understanding, not a one-off project. External consultants can assist by documenting elements, building internal skills and maturity, and challenging groupthink, but the core work must remain in-house.

## Key Takeaways
- EA is a pervasive practice and everyone's responsibility throughout the organization, akin to strategy, demanding long-term internal ownership.
- Avoid consultancies promising to "do your architecture" entirely, as they can only document content, not embody true architecture.
- Consultants' value lies in developing organizational capability to manage complexity, fostering practices for purposeful integration, and providing an external perspective against biases like "not invented here."
- Outsourcing EA fully fails because it ignores local context, personal relationships, and sustained accountability.
